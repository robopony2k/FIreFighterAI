# Game Design

## Design Principles
- **Systems-First**: Mechanics driven by simulation, not scripts.
- **Readability Over Realism**: Simplify complex fire physics for player understanding (e.g., heat diffusion approximates spread).
- **Long-Term Consequences**: Budget and approval changes persist across seasons, emphasizing strategy.
- **Emergent Gameplay**: Player choices create varied outcomes without hard-coded events.

## Design Goals
- Balance realism (fuel-based spread, climate impact) with accessibility (clear UI, phase structure).
- Encourage preparation: Maintenance phase rewards proactive planning.
- High cognitive load in Fire phase to simulate chaos, with recovery for reflection.

## Seasonal Intent
- **Growth**: Passive ecological recovery; players observe but don't intervene directly.
- **Maintenance**: Active preparation; cut breaks, train units to mitigate future fires.
- **Fire**: Chaotic response; deploy units strategically to contain spread.
- **Budget**: Reflective review; allocate based on performance, with approval penalties for failure.

## Player Affordances
- **Growth**: View forecasts, plan ahead.
- **Maintenance**: Deploy units, cut breaks, train crews.
- **Fire**: Click-to-deploy, retask units, monitor spread.
- **Budget**: Adjust allocations, view reports.

## Constraints and Philosophy
- **What Should Never Happen**: Fires in water tiles; units ignoring terrain costs; infinite fuel loops.
- **Abstractions**: Fire spread uses simplified heat diffusion (not full physics); units have abstracted suppression rates.
- **Realism Trade-Offs**: Prioritize fun and pacing over hyper-accurate wildfire models.
